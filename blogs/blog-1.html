A-Ivory Towers

Thoughts about the nature of contemporary discourse over AI Implications relating to and for humans.

It’s not often that you find the frontrunners of an revolution (to borrow a pundit phrase) in front of the wagon they were towing for years, now turning back trying to slow it down and do it in rather public settings.

The life-long intellectual endeavor and fame of these individuals can be questioned, rather, this very commitment towards the advancements in the field is what makes these personalities the punching bag for criticisms from across the spectrum when they present their reservations, albeit a lot of valid ones.

The Zeitgeist

First things first, there’s a lot of nuance to the topic, it’s not new at its core, and people have been debating the possibility as well as the implications of such entities for centuries. What is new is the apparent or possible existence of them in the form the complex AI systems.

The question of there being equivalent agents with intelligence at par or even surpassing human intelligence is not new, religions, for instance, have a lot of them, be it Shaytan (satan), Angels, or Jinns, and their equivalents from other Abrahamic or non-Abrahamic faiths. Though rooted in naturalistic assumptions about the nature of reality, AI seems to be eerily close. If I, somehow haven’t got your eyes rolled just by referencing religion, God bless you ig.

Contemporary zeitgeist is a spectrum of positions, some seem to take the “AI has the potential for good for humans, it should be done cautiously, but don’t regulate it” position, sounds reductionist ?, ik, however, I’d argue this is the core philosophy, which I don’t find convincing at all, more on this in later blogs.

Some seem to take a more cautionary approach, often done injustice with doomsday headline articles, on a deeper reading though, this is unlike what I have read people on LinkedIn handwave as “doomers”, folks in this camp don’t exactly advocate for an AI halt, rather it’s more about a wager I think, an informed one, but a wager nevertheless.
